The problem

Billing is the bottleneck that won't scale

The monthly billing cycle is highly manual, sensitive to upstream data quality, and consumes a disproportionate share of senior billing-team time. Leadership is convinced the current model won't scale economically as Meridian grows.

  • Backup documents arrive in inconsistent formats, ordering, and quality.
  • Three-way reconciliation between draft invoice, transaction report, and documents is line-by-line in Excel.
  • The same exceptions repeat cycle after cycle — re-decided from scratch each time.
  • Instructions are scattered across SAP notes, email, Teams, ServiceNow tickets, and analyst heads.
  • Any approved adjustment triggers full re-validation of the entire invoice.
Process at a glance

Where the time goes today

Draft invoices already exist in SAP. The case begins after the draft, and ends with a delivered invoice.

  • Cost & time capture — staff time and expenses accumulate as unbilled transactions.
  • Draft generation — produced by analyst or scheduled batch job in SAP.
  • Backup collation — pull receipts, vendor invoices, hotel folios, mileage from SharePoint.
  • Reconciliation — match invoice ↔ transaction report ↔ documents, annotate discrepancies.
  • Exception triage — investigate anomalies; chase context across channels.
  • Two-stage review — Project Finance Analyst, then Project Lead.
  • Adjustments → release → archive — all manual in SAP.

Most analyst time is spent on steps 3–7. Time-and-materials invoices drive almost all of that load.

Your mission

By end of day, your team will…

  • Design the target process end-to-end — built to achieve or exceed the KPI targets.
  • Pick one or two focus areas and build a runnable prototype on Appendix A data: document handling · exception triage · reconciliation · instruction & feedback capture.
  • Build the business case — investment, run cost, value, payback, regional sensitivity.
  • Pitch it in a 5–7 slide deck with a live (or recorded) demo.
Non-negotiables

Business requirements

  • SAP is the system of record. Your solution integrates with SAP — it does not replace it.
  • SAP holds financial data — any update to it must be risk-free. Mistaken or unauthorised changes are not acceptable.
  • Project Leads remain undisturbed during this initiative.
  • Compliance and audit traceability are non-negotiable.
  • Achieve or exceed the KPI targets.
  • No real client data. Use only Appendix A or your own synthetic equivalents.
